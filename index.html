Aphantasia Study

Participant Information Sheet

Study title:

Knowing without seeing: A Quantitative Study of Memory Storage in Aphantasia.

You are being invited to take part in a research study. Before deciding, it is essential to understand why the research is being conducted and what it entails. Please take time to read the following information carefully. Please ask the lead researcher if anything is unclear or if you require further information. The researcher's details are included on this form, should you wish to ask any questions before taking part.

Thank you for reading this.

What is the purpose of the study?

This study explores how people remember information from a short narrative, with a particular focus on individuals with aphantasia – a naturally occurring variation in which people do not experience voluntary visual imagery. Aphantasia is not a disorder, learning difficulty, or deficiency. This study looks at how people remember two types of information from a short story. Semantic information refers to the meaning of what you read, such as facts, events, or relationships between characters. Sensory information refers to details related to the senses, such as colours, shapes or visual features. People differ naturally in how vividly they imagine sensory details, and aphantasia is simply one form of this normal variation. The study takes approximately 20-25 minutes to complete and is conducted entirely online.

Why have I been chosen?

You have been chosen because you are: aged 18 or over, fluent in English and either identify as having aphantasia or as having typical visual imagery. Both groups are essential for comparison. The study aims to recruit approximately 50 adults in total.

Do I have to take part?

It is up to you to decide whether to take part. If you decide to participate, you will be given this information sheet to keep and asked to sign a consent form. If you decide to participate, you are still free to withdraw without giving a reason. You may withdraw from the study at any point before submitting your responses, but not afterwards. This can be done simply by closing the browser window; no explanation is required. Because the study collects no identifiable information, it will not be possible to remove individual data after submission, as the researcher cannot link any response back to a specific participant. You may choose to skip any question you do not wish to answer, except for the initial consent items, which must be completed to begin the study.

What will happen if I decide to take part?

If you choose to take part you will:

  • Read and provide digital consent.
  • Answer brief demographic questions (age category, gender, language background).
  • Complete the Vividness of Visual Imagery Questionnaire
  • Read a short, neutral narrative (150-200 words).
  • Complete a brief distractor task.
  • Answer multiple-choice memory questions about the narrative
  • Provide short ratings about your experience
  • Read a debrief explaining the study's aims, with signposts to more information

The entire procedure will take around 20-25 minutes to complete.

What are the potential benefits and risks of taking part?

Although there are no direct benefits to you if you participate, taking part will provide a greater understanding of Aphantasia, which may ultimately benefit researchers understand how different people experience and use mental imagery. There are no risks to you in taking part outside of those you would experience in everyday life. However, by taking part, you may remember things that you may find upsetting. If this occurs, the researcher will ask if you wish to continue participating in the interview. Any decision you make will be respected.

Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

Yes. All the information about your participation in this study will be kept confidential. The anonymous survey will be stored on a password-protected computer only accessible to the researcher. Only the researcher and their supervisor working on the project will have access to the information, which may then be subject to external examination. You will not be identifiable in any report as the data will be grouped together, and all identifying information will be removed.

What will happen at the end of the research study?

Your anonymous data will be analysed using standard statistical methods to compare memory performance between groups. The findings will be written up as part of a dissertation submitted for the partial fulfilment of a Master's degree in Psychology of Mental Health and Wellbeing. A summary of the results may also be shared in academic settings such as presentations or future research publications, but no individual participant will ever be identifiable. The data will be stored confidentially for the current academic year plus six additional years as per the university policy.
